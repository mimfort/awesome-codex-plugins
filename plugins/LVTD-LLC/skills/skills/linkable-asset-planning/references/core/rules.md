# Linkable Asset Planning Rules

Use these rules when creating or reviewing linkable asset ideas.

## Core Rules

### 1. Start With Usefulness

Plan assets that help a specific audience complete a task, understand a topic, evaluate options, or access expertise.

- Define the user's problem.
- Define the publisher's reason to cite it.
- Keep the asset useful even without search traffic.

### 2. Audit Before Creating

Find what already exists before recommending new production.

- Review current site sections and historical assets.
- Pull link evidence for pages that already earned links.
- Look for assets that need better packaging, internal links, design, or promotion.

### 3. Let The Market Calibrate The Idea

Use competitor and publisher link patterns to understand what the market values.

- Analyze most-linked pages, not only top-ranking pages.
- Record format, audience, topic, structure, and likely linker reason.
- Improve on proven formats rather than cloning them.

### 4. Add Value To Aggregation

Resource lists need a framework, not just links.

- Add categories, selection criteria, summaries, scoring, process steps, or context.
- Explain who each resource is for.
- Keep the list maintainable.

### 5. Match Format To Resources

Choose the format the organization can execute well.

- Experts support interviews, guides, commentary, and webinars.
- Data supports reports, charts, maps, and digital PR.
- Developers support calculators, tools, widgets, and templates.
- Products support trials, reviews, contests, or donations only when the value is genuine.

## Red Flags

- The asset is just a product or sales page.
- The idea does not name likely linkers.
- The plan copies competitor content without adding new value.
- The asset exists only to attract links and sends unqualified traffic.
- Giveaways or discounts are the main reason someone would link.
- The conversion path overwhelms the asset's usefulness.
