# Linkable Asset Planning Examples

Examples for turning market evidence into cite-worthy assets.

## Asset Format Selection

| Situation | Asset Format | Why It Fits |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Many scattered resources in the niche | Curated resource hub | Saves the publisher's audience time and adds structure |
| Common repeated calculation or process | Calculator, worksheet, or template | Helps users complete a task |
| Expert community has strong opinions | Expert survey or group interview | Creates original content and participant promotion |
| Market has outdated guides | Updated comprehensive guide | Gives curators a fresher reference |
| Brand has proprietary data | Data study or visual report | Gives journalists and publishers something new to cite |
| Product is useful for community groups | Donation, contest, or review program | Works when value is genuine and disclosure is handled |

## Weak To Strong Asset Framing

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Write a blog post about our product." | "Create a buyer-neutral checklist that helps teams evaluate this product category." |
| "Make a top 100 list." | "Create a categorized directory with selection criteria, summaries, and update cadence." |
| "Publish an infographic." | "Use proprietary data to visualize a market pattern publishers already cover." |
| "Give away product for backlinks." | "Offer product access to relevant community groups and document sponsorship/review rules clearly." |

## Asset Brief Example

For a local services company:

- Linker audience: local parent groups, schools, city blogs.
- Pain: families need seasonal activity planning.
- Asset: city-specific activity calendar with printable checklist.
- Value add: local categories, age ranges, cost filters, accessibility notes.
- Prospects: school newsletters, parent blogs, neighborhood associations, local event pages.
- Conversion path: email reminder signup, not a hard-sell booking form.
