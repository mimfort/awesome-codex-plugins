# Linkable Asset Planning Knowledge

Core concepts for planning content, tools, data, and other assets that publishers can cite.

## Key Concepts

### Link-Worthy Usefulness

Useful, topic-specific resources are the foundation of earned links. A site becomes more link-worthy when it helps people complete tasks, understand topics, compare options, access data, or use tools.

### Linker-Valued Audience Fit

A linkable asset should serve the audience of the publisher who might link to it. The planning question is: what would this publisher be comfortable sending readers to?

### Linkable Asset

A linkable asset can be a web page, tool, data set, expert contribution, relationship, offer, or resource that gives another publisher a reason to cite, share, embed, review, or recommend it.

### Asset Inventory

Inspect existing assets before creating new ones.

| Asset Source | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| On-site | Guides, tools, calculators, data, images, PDFs, videos, podcasts, career pages, events, coupons, product education, resource hubs |
| Organizational | Experts, PR assets, developers, partners, vendors, customers, conference material, training, product samples |
| Market evidence | Competitor pages with links, publisher resource pages, most-linked guides, surveys, and tools |

### Market Pains

Linkable assets often solve recurring market pains. Pain-point research turns repeated "how to," "tips," "guide," "template," "PDF," "video," or "tool" needs into asset categories, sections, tools, or expert prompts.
