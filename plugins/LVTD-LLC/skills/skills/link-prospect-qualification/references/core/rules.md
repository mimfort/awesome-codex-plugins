# Link Prospect Qualification Rules

Use these rules when scoring or filtering link prospects.

## Hard Reject Gates

Reject before scoring when:

- No topical or audience relevance exists.
- The site is a direct competitor and no partnership rationale exists.
- The page exists primarily to sell, swap, or manipulate links.
- The site is an obvious link network, mass directory, article farm, or PBN.
- Malware, cloaking, deceptive UX, or severe ad overload is present.
- The promoted asset is unavailable, weak, orphaned, or not ready for outreach.

## Scoring Rubric

Use 0-5 for each category, then weight by importance.

| Category | Weight | Strong Signal |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Relevance | 25 | Page, audience, and asset tightly match |
| Trust and intent | 20 | Clear human/editorial/resource purpose |
| Editorial quality | 15 | Maintained, human-written, useful, readable |
| Authority and reach | 15 | Strong links, audience, distribution, or recognized publication |
| Outreach fit | 10 | Clear contact path and pitch angle |
| Low spam risk | 10 | Natural profile and no manipulation signals |
| Asset readiness | 5 | Asset is internally connected and shareable |

## Priority Bands

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 85-100 | Priority outreach |
| 70-84 | Standard outreach |
| 55-69 | Nurture or manual review |
| 40-54 | Low priority; special cases only |
| 0-39 | Reject |

## Red Flags

- Authority metrics outrank relevance and intent.
- Existing outbound links look paid, excessive, or unrelated.
- The site has thin text, poor grammar, heavy ads, or stale updates.
- The contact path is unclear and no editor/owner is identifiable.
- The prospect is kept only because competitors have a link there.
- Nofollow/follow ratio is used as the main spam signal.
