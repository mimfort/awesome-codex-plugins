# Link Prospect Qualification Knowledge

Core concepts for deciding which link prospects deserve outreach.

## Key Concepts

### Two-Stage Qualification

Use automated signals to reduce large lists, then manually review the survivors. Automated data helps triage; it should not approve a prospect by itself.

### Relevance

The target page, site audience, and link context must plausibly match the promoted asset. Strong prospects have a clear reason to include the asset for their users.

### Trust And Intent

Trust originates with human stewardship: an editor, author, curator, institution, reporter, or community serving users. A page that exists to sell, swap, or manipulate links is weak even if metrics look attractive.

### Editorial Quality

Good prospects show human-written content, maintained pages, accessible contacts, useful outbound links, low ad friction, and real audience value.

### Spam Risk

Warning signs include link networks, mass directories, reciprocal schemes, paid-link footprints, syndicated article farms, abnormal authority patterns, suspicious link decay, and thin automated content.

### Internal Readiness

The promoted asset should not be orphaned or siloed. It should be findable, internally connected, shareable, and supported by related content.
