# Guest Post Placement Rules

Use these rules when planning or reviewing guest post placements.

## Core Rules

### 1. Name The Audience First

Translate keywords into audience categories.

- Define who the article serves.
- Define the task, problem, or decision they face.
- Use audience terms in prospecting and title design.

### 2. Confirm Citability

Do not pitch a link unless it helps the article.

- Link to data, definitions, methods, tools, examples, downloads, visuals, or expert material.
- For sales pages, add a specific citable section before outreach.
- Support major claims with sources beyond the client.

### 3. Judge Opportunity Quality

Volume is not quality.

- Review editorial process, audience fit, reach, community, backlinks, outbound behavior, and anchor patterns.
- Avoid obvious guest-post networks and paid-post footprints unless risk is explicitly accepted.

### 4. Confirm Expertise And Approvals

Do not promise high-quality placements without the inputs needed.

- Confirm SME access, byline ownership, approval path, social support, and do-not-contact restrictions.
- Match title ideas to available expertise and publisher standards.

### 5. Track Content Inventory

Prevent duplicate placement and reporting confusion.

- Track title, status, prospective domain, placed URL, byline, citation target, and promotion actions.
- Retire placed content from active pitching.

## Red Flags

- Target page is only commercial and has no citable element.
- Guest post title is not tailored to publisher audience.
- User wants to attach finished articles in first-touch scaled outreach.
- Existing guest posts on the site are low-quality or link-farm-like.
- The campaign lacks enough approved authors or experts.
