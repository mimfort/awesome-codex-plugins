# Plan Local Sponsorship Campaign Workflow

Use this workflow to plan, prospect, evaluate, fulfill, and measure local sponsorship link-building campaigns.

## When to Use

- The user wants local sponsorship links.
- A local SEO campaign needs community visibility.
- Sponsorship packages need evaluation.
- Existing local relationships could create link opportunities.

## Prerequisites

- Target geography.
- Business and local SEO goals.
- Budget and timeline.
- Brand restrictions and payment path.

**Reference**: `references/core/knowledge.md`, `references/core/rules.md`

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Set Campaign Parameters

- [ ] Capture target city, county, neighborhoods, ZIP codes, and priority service areas.
- [ ] Define primary and secondary goals.
- [ ] Set budget, max sponsorship spend, target count, deadline, and approvals.
- [ ] Define must-have and nice-to-have benefits.
- [ ] Collect brand restrictions and payment logistics.

### Step 2: Prospect Opportunities

- [ ] Search sponsor, donor, partner, event, and category queries.
- [ ] Add audience/category modifiers.
- [ ] Include seasonal events two to three months before event timing when useful.
- [ ] Build backups beyond the desired final count.

### Step 3: Outreach And Data Collection

- [ ] Introduce the sponsor briefly and ask one clear question.
- [ ] Use calls only for high-fit or deadline-sensitive prospects.
- [ ] Track organization, URL, location, contact, cost, benefits, sponsor page, PDF, notes, and status.
- [ ] Allow response time before final selection.

### Step 4: Evaluate And Select

- [ ] Score each opportunity with the rubric.
- [ ] Confirm benefits, timing, link target, and fulfillment reliability.
- [ ] Recommend a ranked shortlist with rationale and risks.

### Step 5: Fulfill And Measure

- [ ] Send asset packet and payment details.
- [ ] Confirm placement dates and benefit deadlines.
- [ ] Verify links, logos, UTMs, social posts, newsletters, coupon codes, booth details, and duration.
- [ ] Track SEO, local reach, direct attribution, branded search, and relationship value.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Do Instead |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Starting outreach before constraints | Causes rejections and delays | Define brand/payment rules first |
| Choosing by DA alone | Misses local value | Score local relevance and benefits |
| Treating sponsorship as link buying | Creates risk and weak relationships | Frame community value |
| No fulfillment check | Paid benefits may not happen | Verify every benefit |

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] Campaign parameters and constraints are clear.
- [ ] Prospecting and outreach plan are ready.
- [ ] Shortlist is scored against local and business goals.
- [ ] Fulfillment and measurement plans are defined.
