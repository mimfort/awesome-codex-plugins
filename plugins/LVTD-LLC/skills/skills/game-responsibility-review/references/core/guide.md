# Game Responsibility Review Guide

## Review Areas

### Player Transformation

- What habits, skills, attitudes, or expectations does the game reinforce?
- Does the game leave players feeling more capable, connected, curious, or depleted?
- Does the design's actual incentive structure match its stated purpose?

### Retention Pressure

- streaks
- daily rewards
- fear of missing out
- limited-time offers
- variable rewards
- notification prompts
- social obligation
- grind gates

### Monetization Pressure

- pay-to-win advantages
- unclear odds
- spending prompts after failure
- friction that can be paid away
- child-directed purchases
- currency obfuscation
- subscriptions that are hard to understand or cancel

### Community Risk

- harassment vectors
- cheating incentives
- griefing
- exclusionary matchmaking
- unsafe user-generated content
- moderation burden
- privacy and identity exposure

### Accessibility And Inclusion

- avoidable sensory overload
- unreadable text or color-only signals
- unremappable controls
- unnecessary time pressure
- inaccessible onboarding
- stereotype-heavy characters or cultures

## Risk Ratings

- `Blocker`: likely harm or legal/platform risk; do not ship without redesign or explicit executive decision.
- `High`: meaningful player harm or trust risk; mitigation required.
- `Medium`: manageable with design changes, settings, or messaging.
- `Low`: monitor after release.

## Better Design Moves

- Replace compulsion with clear value.
- Make odds, costs, and consequences legible.
- Give players control over notifications and intensity.
- Let players recover from failure without coercive payment.
- Add accessibility options early enough to influence mechanics.
- Design moderation and reporting before community scale.
