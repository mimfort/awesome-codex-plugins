# Book Reader Conversations Checklist

Use this checklist before and after a reader conversation round.

## Before The Round

- [ ] Target reader hypothesis is specific.
- [ ] Book promise hypothesis is written in one or two sentences.
- [ ] Learning goal is defined.
- [ ] Conversation mode is selected: listening or teaching.
- [ ] Recruiting list contains readers close to the target profile.
- [ ] Prompt avoids asking whether the book idea is good.
- [ ] Note-taking schema is ready.

## During Conversations

- [ ] Ask for recent concrete experiences.
- [ ] Ask what the reader tried before.
- [ ] Ask where they searched for help.
- [ ] Ask what was confusing, frustrating, risky, or not worth it.
- [ ] Capture exact reader language.
- [ ] Avoid defending the idea.
- [ ] In teaching mode, help the reader receive real value.

## After The Round

- [ ] Group signals by reader profile.
- [ ] Identify repeated words and phrases.
- [ ] Identify urgency or lack of urgency.
- [ ] Identify missing prerequisites.
- [ ] Identify weak or misunderstood promise wording.
- [ ] Identify TOC changes.
- [ ] Identify potential beta readers.
- [ ] Decide the next test.

## Red Flags

- Readers politely say it sounds interesting but do not ask anything.
- The author explains the promise differently every time.
- Readers care about adjacent problems more than the stated promise.
- Readers cannot remember or repeat who the book is for.
- Teaching conversations repeatedly require improvised missing steps.
- The author argues with reader confusion instead of revising the book.

## Quality Rubric

| Dimension | Strong | Weak |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Reader fit | Recently faced the problem | Abstract interest |
| Evidence | Stories and behavior | Opinions and predictions |
| Language | Exact phrases captured | Generic paraphrase only |
| Synthesis | Book decisions made | Transcript summarized |
| Next step | Sharper test chosen | More vague conversations |
