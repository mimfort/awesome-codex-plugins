# Game Balance Economy Guide

## Balance Dimensions

- Fairness: players understand why outcomes happened and feel rules apply consistently.
- Challenge: success requires effort without becoming unreadable or exhausting.
- Meaningful choice: options differ in consequence, not just presentation.
- Skill versus chance: randomness creates uncertainty without replacing mastery.
- Reward cadence: rewards arrive often enough to teach and motivate.
- Punishment: failure has consequence without forcing avoidable repetition.
- Economy: resources enter and leave at rates that support the desired pace.
- Progression: power, complexity, and mastery grow coherently.

## Economy Map

For every currency or resource, define:

- Source: how it enters the game.
- Sink: how it leaves the game.
- Storage: whether players can hoard it.
- Conversion: whether it can become another resource.
- Gate: what it unlocks or blocks.
- Feedback: how the player knows its value changed.
- Failure case: what happens if it becomes too abundant or too scarce.

## Common Problems

- Dominant strategy: one choice beats alternatives across most contexts.
- False choice: options are presented but consequences barely differ.
- Runaway leader: early success compounds until the outcome is decided too soon.
- Rubber banding mismatch: help for trailing players feels artificial or unfair.
- Randomness opacity: players cannot tell whether skill mattered.
- Grind: optimal play repeats low-interest actions for too long.
- Reward inflation: rewards must constantly grow to feel meaningful.
- Economy leak: a source has no matching sink.
- Punishment spiral: failure removes the tools needed to recover.

## Accessibility And Difficulty

Difficulty settings should expose real tuning levers rather than only changing hidden multipliers. Consider options for timing windows, enemy aggression, information clarity, failure penalty, assistive targeting, skip/retry structure, and resource abundance.
