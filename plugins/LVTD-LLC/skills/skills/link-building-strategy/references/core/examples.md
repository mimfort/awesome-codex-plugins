# Link Building Strategy Examples

Examples for choosing a campaign direction.

## Campaign Template Fit

| Situation | Better Campaign | Why |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| Market has many scattered guides, PDFs, and tools | Resource aggregation | Adds value by organizing and contextualizing fragmented resources |
| Active expert community with strong opinions | Expert interview or survey | Creates relationship value and participant-driven promotion |
| Brand has proprietary data | Digital PR or data-led content | Gives publishers a newsworthy reason to cite the brand |
| Product can be donated to relevant groups | Contest, donation, or sponsorship | Works when community benefit is real and disclosure/risk are handled |
| Existing high-value content lacks exposure | Resource page outreach or roundup promotion | The asset already has a cite-worthy reason to be included |

## Weak Framing To Strong Framing

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "We need backlinks to our pricing page." | "We need links to a comparison resource that helps buyers evaluate pricing models, then routes qualified readers to pricing." |
| "Let's do guest posts." | "We have expert practitioners and a citable guide, so guest placements can build expertise and naturally reference the guide." |
| "Find high-DA sites." | "Find authoritative publishers whose audiences need this asset and whose pages already cite similar resources." |
| "Create a big list post." | "Create a curated resource hub with categories, selection criteria, summaries, and outreach to included sources." |

## Metric Example

For a resource-page campaign, report:

- Qualified prospects researched.
- Prospects rejected with reasons.
- Outreach replies and placements.
- New referring domains to target URL.
- Referral sessions and conversions.
- Target-page query and ranking movement.
- New relationships or future content opportunities.
