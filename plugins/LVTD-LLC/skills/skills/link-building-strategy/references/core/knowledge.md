# Link Building Strategy Knowledge

Core concepts for designing link-building campaigns that can compound.

## Key Concepts

### Sustainable Campaign Scope

A campaign should be designed around the organization's strengths, resources, approvals, and goals. If all process, relationships, or expertise live outside the organization, link momentum often stops when the vendor or temporary campaign stops.

Scope the campaign around:

- Existing channels, subscribers, communities, relationships, offers, data, tools, and experts.
- Business goals such as authority, rankings, leads, brand, local visibility, or market education.
- Linkable assets that publishers would cite or share.
- Publisher ecosystems and opportunity types in the market.
- Departments needed for approval and execution.
- Available time, budget, expertise, and production capacity.

### Link Strategist Role

The strategist owns the design phase: market research, campaign mission, process, task list, metrics, and iteration. This role should have enough time to research linker audiences and develop campaign ideas instead of only sending outreach.

### Linker-Valued Audiences

Linkable content serves an audience that a likely linker cares about. This audience may differ from the company's buyers. A campaign should state what publisher audience is helped and why a publisher would be proud to send readers to the asset.

### Publisher Types

| Publisher Type | Usually Values |
|----------------|----------------|
| News, blog, and community publishers | Stories, data, expert opinion, useful guides, trends, and audience relevance |
| Resource page curators | Comprehensive, evergreen, credible resources for a specific audience or task |
| Industry publications | News, analysis, survey results, case studies, expert interviews, and process advances |
| Local/community publishers | Local relevance, community benefit, sponsor or event value, and useful local resources |

### Campaign Metrics

Link count alone is not a strategy. Measure the impact the campaign is designed to create.

| Metric Layer | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| SEO | Referring domains, relevant links, authority signals, rankings, nonbrand traffic, geo visibility |
| Outreach | Reply rate, qualified replies, placements, follow-ups, relationship history |
| Market engagement | Referral traffic, mentions, social shares, expert participation, branded search |
| Business | Leads, signups, sales, assisted conversions, partner opportunities, content reuse |
