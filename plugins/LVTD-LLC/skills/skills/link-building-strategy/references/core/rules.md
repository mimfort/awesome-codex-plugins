# Link Building Strategy Rules

Use these rules when designing or reviewing a link-building campaign.

## Core Rules

### 1. Start With The Business Outcome

Define what link building should support before choosing tactics.

- Rankings, links, traffic, and authority are intermediate metrics.
- Tie the campaign to leads, conversions, brand lift, local visibility, market education, relationship building, or a specific page's organic growth.
- Reject goals that cannot be measured or explained to stakeholders.

### 2. Build From Existing Strengths

Use assets and channels the organization already has before inventing a new campaign.

- Look for experts, proprietary data, tools, relationships, subscribers, products, events, communities, and PR assets.
- Choose tactics the team can sustain with available resources.
- Keep approvals within the smallest group that can execute when possible.

### 3. Design For Linker-Valued Audiences

Create assets for the audience a publisher serves, not only for buyers.

- Name the linker segment and their readers.
- State why linking improves the publisher's page.
- Avoid sales-first content when the linker needs reference value, news, data, or audience utility.

### 4. Choose Tactics After Fit Is Clear

Select campaign templates only after asset, audience, publisher, and resource fit are understood.

- Match resource aggregation to markets with fragmented useful resources.
- Match expert surveys to active expert communities.
- Match digital PR to credible brands with newsworthy data or exclusives.
- Match sponsorships or giveaways to real community value, not disguised link purchase.

### 5. Measure Multiple Layers

Set metrics before launch.

- Include leading indicators such as qualified prospects, replies, placements, and mentions.
- Include SEO indicators such as relevant referring domains, target-page movement, and nonbrand traffic.
- Include business indicators such as referral conversions, leads, and brand demand.

## Red Flags

- The plan only says "get more links."
- The tactic was chosen because a tool or agency is optimized for it.
- No target linker audience is named.
- The asset is not useful without SEO value.
- Metrics are only rankings, link count, or domain authority.
- Every department must approve before the campaign can move.
- The campaign cannot continue after the initial contractor stops.
