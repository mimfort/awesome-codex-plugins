# Design Link-Building Campaign Workflow

Use this workflow to create or revise a link-building campaign brief.

## When to Use

- The user asks for a link-building strategy or campaign.
- The user has a link goal but no campaign scope.
- The user is choosing between outreach, guest posts, resource pages, digital PR, sponsorships, or other tactics.

## Prerequisites

- Website or business description.
- Target pages or assets if known.
- Business goal and SEO goal.
- Available content, PR, expert, product, design, and engineering resources.

**Reference**: `references/core/knowledge.md`, `references/core/rules.md`

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Clarify The Outcome

**Goal**: Convert "get links" into a measurable campaign outcome.

- [ ] Name the business outcome.
- [ ] Name the SEO outcome.
- [ ] Identify the target page, asset, or section.
- [ ] Define the review date and success indicators.

### Step 2: Inventory Strengths And Constraints

**Goal**: Build from what the organization can actually use.

- [ ] List existing assets, data, tools, experts, products, events, and relationships.
- [ ] List available budget, production capacity, and approval owners.
- [ ] Identify constraints: legal, brand, compliance, local markets, sensitive verticals, or do-not-contact lists.

### Step 3: Map Linker Audiences And Publishers

**Goal**: Define who might link and who they serve.

- [ ] Identify linker-valued audiences.
- [ ] Map publisher types: resource curators, industry publications, bloggers, journalists, communities, local groups, partners.
- [ ] State why each publisher type would care.

### Step 4: Choose The Campaign Type

**Goal**: Match tactic to asset, audience, opportunity, and resources.

- [ ] Compare at least 2-4 viable campaign templates.
- [ ] Select the best fit.
- [ ] Record rejected alternatives and why they are weaker now.

### Step 5: Define The Asset And Prospecting Plan

**Goal**: Make the campaign actionable.

- [ ] Define what asset must exist or be improved.
- [ ] Define opportunity types and prospect sources.
- [ ] Define qualification gates and outreach angle.
- [ ] Identify cross-skill handoffs: asset planning, prospecting, qualification, outreach, or tactic-specific skills.

### Step 6: Set Metrics And Risks

**Goal**: Make performance review credible.

- [ ] Define outreach metrics.
- [ ] Define SEO metrics.
- [ ] Define referral, relationship, and business metrics.
- [ ] List spam, compliance, approval, quality, and resource risks.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Do Instead |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Picking tactics first | Creates asset and audience mismatch | Start with goal, assets, and publishers |
| Measuring links only | Misses business and relationship value | Use layered metrics |
| Serving buyers only | Publishers may not care | Serve linker-valued audiences |
| Ignoring approvals | Campaign stalls after research | Identify owners early |

## Exit Criteria

Task is complete when:

- [ ] The brief names goal, asset, linker audience, tactic, metrics, and risks.
- [ ] The tactic has a clear fit with available resources.
- [ ] The next action can be assigned to asset planning, prospecting, qualification, or outreach.
