# Classify Meeting Outcome

Use this workflow to interpret meeting notes, CRM entries, or a founder's
summary of how a customer, investor, partner, or sales meeting ended.

## Steps

- [ ] Extract the exact ending: what was promised, scheduled, introduced, paid,
  signed, or deferred.
- [ ] Identify the currency given up: time, reputation, money, or none.
- [ ] Mark the outcome as commitment, advancement, rejection, weak interest, or
  bad data.
- [ ] If the outcome is weak, propose one concrete ask that can happen now.
- [ ] If the outcome is rejection, write what was learned and what assumption it
  changes.
- [ ] If the outcome is commitment, define owner, deadline, and follow-up.
- [ ] If the next step has no owner, date, or customer-side effort, downgrade it.

## Interpretation Hints

| Signal | Likely Meaning |
|--------|----------------|
| "Keep me posted" | Weak interest unless tied to a specific action. |
| Intro to decision maker | Reputation commitment and advancement. |
| Trial with no setup cost | Weak until they invest real effort or data. |
| Paid pilot or deposit | Strong commitment. |
| Rejection after concrete ask | Useful evidence. |

## Output

Return: classification, evidence, currency given up, what it proves, what it
does not prove, and the next concrete ask.

## Exit Criteria

The team knows whether the meeting changed the pipeline, only felt positive, or
needs a sharper next ask.
